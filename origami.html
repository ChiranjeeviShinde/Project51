This is a CAMEL


Some facts about camels
  • Camels have three sets of eyelids and two rows of eyelashes to keep sand out of their eyes.
  • Camels have thick lips which let them forage for thorny plants other animals can't eat.
  • Camels can completely shut their nostrils during sandstorms

This is a CHAMELEON


Some facts about chameleons
  • Chameleons don't change colors to camouflage themselves.
  • They use their toes and tails to get around.
  • Chameleons come in a wide range of sizes.

This is a PIGEON


Some facts about pigeons
  • Pigeons are incredibly complex and intelligent animals.
  • Pigeons are renowned for their outstanding navigational abilities.
  • Pigeons are highly sociable animals.


This is a TEDDY BEAR

Some facts about teddies
  • Teddy bears got their name from the story that Teddy Roosevelt refused to shoot a bear cub while on a hunting trip in 1902.
  • The first toy stuffed bear was created by German toymaker Margarete Steiff.
  • The Oxford English Dictionary dates the first use of the term teddy bear to 1906.

This is a PANDA

Some facts about pandas
  • They have great camouflage for their environment.
  • They spend a lot of their day eating.
  • Their eyes are different to normal bears.

This is a FLYING CICADA

Some facts about flying cicadas
  • Cicadas are not locusts.
  • Cicadas have one of the longest insect lifespans.
  • Cicadas inundate forests as a survival mechanism.
